My Gear


Nektar Impact GX49

When I was younger, I composed music in the worst way possible. I used my hand, dragged the mouse to get the note-length I wanted, dragged the mouse onto the staff, and then clicked. For any of you who have tried to engrave with such method, you know how much it sucks. I was unaware that there were other ways of using composition software. My Freshman year of college is when I finally got a midi keyboard. 24 keys were all I had to work with. I want to emphasize the freedom I gained from using 49 keys. My speed of composition doubled, and my harmonic vocabulary exploded. This low-cost piece of technology was well worth the investment. It's been my composition companion for years, and I hope for it to be yours as well.


Yamaha Trombone

Let me tell you a story. My Christmas list when I was 16 consisted of a brand new trombone. I got a Mendini trombone from amazon. Imagine this, you get a brand new trombone, open up the case, piece together the instrument, and then play your first note on it. You expect a tone you're used to, but instead get the dirtiest, gross beam of sound your ears have ever witnessed. Imagine my shock when I learned that a trombone costing only a few bucks would sound like that. I was of course grateful for the instrument and used it for many years. Let me tell you, the difference that happens when you get yourself a nice trombone that actually sounds good is huge. I highly highly recommend you get yourself a better trombone. I've been using this one for years and it has never let me down.


Bach 5G Mouthpiece

Other than having internet with your trombone, the 5G mouthpiece will give you an amazing tone. When my trombone lesson professor first recommended a big mouthpiece like this I didn't think I'd be able to play any high notes. But alas, to my surprise, I was able to hit all sorts of higher notes with amazing tone quality. Gone were the days of laser tone. Eventually I found it even easier to hit higher notes with this mouthpiece. Trust me, this mouthpiece has helped me in so many ways.


Finale

Not really gear, but definitely something that has been by my side since I was 11. When I got my first instrument, the cello, I received a packet on how to play the cello that came with a CD. The CD included the Devil's spawn, Smart-Music, as well as finale notepad. I used the latter program over and over once I learned that it can be used to write music. To this day I use finale, and I've mastered the art of engraving with it. I love it for its versatility, playback options, and light weight. I've tried the other big name composition software and just can't get behind them like I can with finale.


Behringer Pre-Amp

A good preamp is literally a pain to find. I have 4, 3 of them suck, and 2 of those sucky ones are very overpriced. After lots of digging I found the U-Phoria preamp and it's been wonderful for me! What exactly do I use this pre-amp for? It's the backbone of all my recording. I plug my microphone into the pre-amp, then plug it into my PC, letting me record everything. The only downside is that there's some fuzz that occurs randomly, but a simple unplug then replug will fix it. It has both a quarter inch input and a mic cable input. It's mono, but honestly that's all I need for what I do. If you're looking for stereo they have that option as well in the link!
