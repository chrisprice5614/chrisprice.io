Ghost Stallion

Written for the Boise Gems Drum and Bugle Corps. The music is based around the legend of a Ghost Stallion who rescues travelers moving through dangerous parts of the mountains. It includes original music, as well as selections from "The Rite of Spring" by Stravinsky. This piece was performed for several audiences accross the pacific northwest, and acted as the Boise Gems' second field show.


With All Thanks

Another piece written for the Boise Gems Drum and Bugle Corps. With All Thanks was originally meant as a thank you letter to those who donated the trailor, and labor for the Gems 2024 uniforms. It's a mixture of Be Thou My Vision and Amazing Grace. This arrangement went on to become the corps song for the Boise Gems. It's played often as a method of giving thanks to anyone who as helped out the Gems in any way.


Esto Perpetua

The first field show written for the Boise Gems Drum and Bugle Corps. The title is Idaho's state motto, meaning "Let it be forever," being a symbol to the Gems Drum and Bugle Corps as a whole. The piece features completely original music divided into 3 parts, "Motion Begins," "Acceleration," and ending with "Esto Perpetua." The music evokes imagery of adventure, success, and happiness.


Fossil Words

Cosmic Clash

Originally stock marching band movement. This piece is meant to tell the story of taking the journey into space.
